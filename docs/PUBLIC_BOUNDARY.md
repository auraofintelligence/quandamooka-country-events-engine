# Public Boundary

The public website should help hosts and community participants understand the event pathway without exposing private planning material.

## Keep public

- public event name
- public host or organiser name when approved
- broad place or venue name when approved
- public dates and times
- booking or registration links
- accessibility information
- transport and visitor guidance
- weather, safety and service updates approved for release
- public thanks and approved sponsor notes
- approved photos or media
- public aftercare summary

## Keep private by default

- private phone numbers and email addresses
- private planning notes
- exact equipment storage locations
- unapproved images or names
- sensitive cultural material
- protected places
- detailed security plans
- incident details that identify people
- medical or welfare details
- financial accounts and payment information
- internal conflict, negotiation or legal notes

## Required wording stance

- Do not imply official authority unless it has been granted.
- Do not treat guessed catalogue data as consent.
- Do not frame one person as director of the ecosystem.
- Do not describe a proposed capability as if it already exists.
- Use clear source status when dates, permissions or listings need confirmation.
